# #08 — ER=EPR and the entanglement-geometry connection

*Also known as: ER=EPR, entanglement is geometry, spacetime from entanglement.*

> **Status: OPEN** · Statement tier: `open_conjecture` · Resolution tier: `open_conjecture` · First posed: 2013.

## The problem

Maldacena and Susskind (2013) conjectured ER=EPR: that an Einstein-Rosen bridge (ER, a wormhole) is dual to quantum entanglement (EPR), so two entangled systems are connected by a (generally non-traversable) geometric bridge. More broadly, the conjecture suggests spacetime connectivity itself emerges from entanglement (Van Raamsdonk; Ryu-Takayanagi). A precise, general, and proven formulation — specifying exactly which entangled states correspond to which geometries, and in what regime — remains open.

## Key ideas

- An eternal AdS black hole is dual to two CFTs in the thermofield-double (maximally entangled) state, connected by a non-traversable wormhole (Maldacena 2001).
- ER=EPR generalizes this: entanglement between any two systems is geometric connectivity.
- Van Raamsdonk: disentangling boundary regions pinches off the bulk, so entanglement 'builds' spacetime.
- Ryu-Takayanagi: entanglement entropy equals the area of a bulk minimal surface — area/entanglement again.
- Traversable wormholes (Gao-Jafferis-Wall) realize controlled information transfer, sharpening ER=EPR.
- Bears on the firewall (#06): identifying interior and exterior partners as the same wormhole endpoints can preserve a smooth horizon.

## Live approaches

- **Holographic entanglement (Ryu-Takayanagi / QES)** (`open_conjecture`) — Geometrize entanglement entropy as minimal-surface area; quantum extremal surfaces extend it. _Proponents: Shinsei Ryu, Tadashi Takayanagi, Netta Engelhardt._
- **Emergent spacetime from entanglement** (`open_conjecture`) — Reconstruct bulk geometry/connectivity from boundary entanglement structure. _Proponents: Mark Van Raamsdonk, Brian Swingle._
- **Traversable wormholes** (`open_conjecture`) — Engineer entanglement (a double-trace coupling) to make the bridge traversable, testing ER=EPR operationally. _Proponents: Ping Gao, Daniel Jafferis, Aron Wall._
