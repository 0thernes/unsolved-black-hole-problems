# #03 — Microscopic origin of Bekenstein-Hawking entropy

*Also known as: black hole entropy microstates, A/4 entropy, statistical origin of horizon entropy.*

> **Status: PARTIALLY SOLVED** · Statement tier: `established_theory` · Resolution tier: `open_conjecture` · First posed: 1972-1973.

## The problem

A black hole carries entropy S = k_B c^3 A / (4 G hbar) — one quarter of its horizon area in Planck units (Bekenstein 1973; Hawking 1975). In ordinary statistical mechanics, entropy counts microstates. What are the microstates of a black hole? String theory (Strominger-Vafa 1996) reproduced S = A/4 by counting D-brane states for special supersymmetric, extremal (and near-extremal) black holes. A microscopic state-counting for a generic astrophysical (e.g. Kerr) black hole, and a framework-independent understanding of why the answer is universally A/4, remain open.

## History & originators

- **Jacob Bekenstein** — 1972-73 proposed that black holes carry entropy proportional to horizon area.
- **Stephen Hawking** — 1975 fixed the coefficient to 1/4 via the radiation temperature (the first law).

## Why it ranks #03

Deep (the meaning of A/4 touches quantum gravity and holography), old (1973), but partially solved: string theory counts microstates exactly for SUSY/extremal cases. The general (Kerr) case and a universal explanation are open.

## Key ideas

- S = A/4 in Planck units is universal across black-hole types — a strong hint about quantum gravity.
- The area law motivated the holographic principle ('t Hooft, Susskind): degrees of freedom scale with area, not volume.
- Strominger-Vafa counted Bogomol'nyi-Prasad-Sommerfield (BPS) D-brane microstates and matched A/4 exactly for extremal charged black holes.
- Loop quantum gravity counts spin-network punctures of the horizon, fixing A/4 via the Barbero-Immirzi parameter.
- Logarithmic corrections to S (Sen and others) provide cross-checks between approaches.
- Open: a microscopic count for a generic (non-extremal, rotating, astrophysical) black hole and a framework-independent derivation of the 1/4.

## Live approaches

- **String theory / D-brane counting** (`established_theory`) — Count BPS microstates of brane bound states; matches A/4 for SUSY extremal and near-extremal black holes. _Proponents: Andrew Strominger, Cumrun Vafa, Ashoke Sen._
- **Loop quantum gravity horizon counting** (`speculative_extension`) — Count quantum-geometry states puncturing the horizon; fixes A/4 via the Barbero-Immirzi parameter. _Proponents: Carlo Rovelli, Abhay Ashtekar, Kirill Krasnov._
- **Euclidean / Noether-charge (Wald) entropy** (`analytic_classical`) — Entropy as a Noether charge of the horizon-generating Killing field; generalizes A/4 to higher-curvature gravity. _Proponents: Robert Wald, Ted Jacobson._
- **Conformal symmetry near the horizon (Cardy)** (`open_conjecture`) — Near-horizon conformal symmetry fixes the state count via Cardy's formula, independent of microscopic details. _Proponents: Steven Carlip, Andrew Strominger._
