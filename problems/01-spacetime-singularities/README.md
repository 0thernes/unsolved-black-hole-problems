# #01 — Spacetime singularities & their quantum-gravity resolution

> **Status: OPEN** (resolution requires quantum gravity) · Statement tier:
> `analytic_classical` · Resolution tier: `open_conjecture` · First posed:
> 1916 (Schwarzschild) / 1965 (Penrose).

## The problem

General relativity predicts that gravitational collapse ends in a **spacetime
singularity** — a place where curvature invariants (e.g. the Kretschmann scalar
`R_{abcd}R^{abcd}`) diverge, geodesics are incomplete, and the classical theory
simply stops making predictions. The **Penrose–Hawking singularity theorems**
show this is *generic*: given an energy condition, a causality condition, and a
**trapped surface**, singularity formation is unavoidable — it is not an
artifact of the perfect spherical symmetry of the Schwarzschild solution. The
open problem: **what actually happens there?** The consensus is that the answer
lies in a quantum theory of gravity, which does not yet exist in complete,
predictive form. This is the deepest and oldest black-hole problem — hence #1.

## History & originators

- **Karl Schwarzschild (1916)** wrote down the first exact solution of Einstein's
  equations weeks after the field equations were published — from the Russian
  front in WWI, where he died months later. His solution already contained the
  `r = 0` curvature singularity (and the `r = 2M` coordinate singularity later
  understood as the horizon).
- **Roger Penrose (1965)** revolutionized the question by importing global,
  topological methods: the concept of a **closed trapped surface** plus the
  Raychaudhuri focusing of null geodesics proves geodesic incompleteness without
  any symmetry assumption. This work — for which Penrose received the **2020
  Nobel Prize in Physics** — turned "singularities" from a suspected artifact
  into a robust prediction of GR.
- **Stephen Hawking** extended the theorems with Penrose to cosmology (the Big
  Bang as a past singularity) and generic collapse.

## Why it is hard

The singularity is exactly where GR's assumptions fail, so GR cannot describe
its own breakdown. A resolution must (a) reduce to classical GR away from the
core, and (b) either evade an assumption of the singularity theorems (e.g.
quantum violation of the energy condition) or replace the singular region with
finite quantum geometry. No candidate theory of quantum gravity is yet complete
and experimentally tested.

## Live approaches (with truth tiers)

- **Loop quantum gravity / quantum bounce** (`speculative_extension`) — discrete
  geometry replaces the singularity with a bounce (Ashtekar, Bojowald).
- **String theory / fuzzballs** (`speculative_extension`) — the interior is a
  horizon-scale quantum bound state with no singular core (Mathur).
- **Regular black holes** (`speculative_extension`) — effective metrics with a
  de Sitter core (Bardeen, Hayward); phenomenological, seeking a fundamental
  basis.
- **Asymptotic safety** and other UV-completion programs.

## What would count as a solution

A predictive, internally-consistent theory of the Planck-scale interior that
reduces to GR outside, makes a falsifiable prediction (e.g. an observable
signature of a bounce or of remnant structure), and is confirmed.
