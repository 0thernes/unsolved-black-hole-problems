# #01 — Spacetime singularities & their quantum-gravity resolution

*Also known as: the singularity problem, breakdown of general relativity, quantum gravity at r=0.*

> **Status: OPEN** · Statement tier: `analytic_classical` · Resolution tier: `open_conjecture` · First posed: 1916-1965.

## The problem

General relativity predicts that gravitational collapse produces a spacetime singularity where curvature invariants diverge and the classical theory breaks down. The Penrose-Hawking theorems prove such singularities are generic (not artifacts of symmetry), given reasonable energy and causality conditions. What physically replaces the singularity is unknown and is believed to require a quantum theory of gravity, which does not yet exist in complete, predictive form.

## History & originators

- **Karl Schwarzschild** — 1916 vacuum solution exhibiting the r=0 curvature singularity (and the coordinate singularity at r=2M).
- **Roger Penrose** — 1965 singularity theorem using trapped surfaces and global methods; Nobel Prize 2020.
- **Stephen Hawking** — extended the theorems (with Penrose) to cosmology and generic collapse.

## Why it ranks #01

The deepest and oldest: resolving it requires the missing theory of quantum gravity. Ranked #1 because no other black-hole problem is more fundamental or has resisted longer.

*(depth 10/10 · age 10/10 · tractability 2/10 — higher tractability pushes the rank toward the bottom of the list.)*

## Key ideas

- Curvature singularities are where geodesics are incomplete and invariants like the Kretschmann scalar diverge.
- Penrose's trapped-surface argument makes singularity formation generic, independent of exact symmetry.
- Cosmic censorship (problems #04, #05) concerns whether such singularities are hidden behind horizons.
- Candidate resolutions replace r=0 with a quantum-gravity core: a bounce (loop quantum gravity), a fuzzball/string condensate, or a regular (non-singular) black-hole interior.
- Any resolution must reduce to classical GR in the appropriate limit and respect the singularity theorems' assumptions or evade one of them (e.g. via quantum energy-condition violation).

## Live approaches

- **Loop quantum gravity / quantum bounce** (`speculative_extension`) — Discrete quantum geometry replaces the singularity with a bounce to an expanding region. _Proponents: Abhay Ashtekar, Martin Bojowald._
- **String theory / fuzzballs** (`speculative_extension`) — The interior is a horizon-scale quantum bound state of strings/branes with no singular core. _Proponents: Samir Mathur._
- **Regular (non-singular) black holes** (`speculative_extension`) — Effective metrics (Bardeen, Hayward) with a de Sitter core; phenomenological, seeking a fundamental basis. _Proponents: James Bardeen, Sean Hayward._
- **Asymptotic safety / other QG programs** (`speculative_extension`) — Running couplings soften the UV behavior of gravity near the core. _Proponents: Steven Weinberg (program)._
