# #02 — The black-hole information paradox

*Also known as: Hawking information paradox, unitarity vs evaporation, information loss problem.*

> **Status: OPEN WITH RECENT PROGRESS** · Statement tier: `established_theory` · Resolution tier: `open_conjecture` · First posed: 1975-1976.

## The problem

Hawking's 1975 calculation shows a black hole emits thermal radiation and slowly evaporates. If the radiation is exactly thermal and the hole disappears completely, an initial pure quantum state evolves into a mixed (thermal) state, violating the unitarity of quantum mechanics and destroying information. Reconciling black-hole evaporation with unitarity is the central problem of quantum gravity's low-energy regime. Recent island/replica-wormhole computations reproduce a unitary Page curve for the radiation entropy, but the explicit mechanism by which information escapes the interior remains open.

## History & originators

- **Stephen Hawking** — 1975 derivation of black-hole radiation; 1976 argument that evaporation breaks predictability (information loss).

## Key ideas

- Hawking radiation is (to leading semiclassical order) thermal, carrying no information about what formed the hole.
- The Page curve: if evaporation is unitary, the radiation's entanglement entropy must rise then fall, peaking at the Page time (~half the entropy radiated).
- Islands / quantum extremal surfaces: regions of the interior counted as part of the radiation's entropy, reproducing the Page curve from a gravitational computation.
- Replica wormholes: subleading gravitational saddles in the Euclidean path integral that supply the entropy turnover.
- Tension with the equivalence principle is sharpened by the firewall argument (problem #06).
- Open core: even with a unitary Page curve, the physical mechanism transferring information from interior to radiation (and the fate of the interior) is not settled.

## Live approaches

- **Islands / quantum extremal surfaces** (`open_conjecture`) — Compute the radiation entropy including 'island' regions; yields the Page curve in semiclassical gravity. _Proponents: Geoffrey Penington, Ahmed Almheiri, Netta Engelhardt, Don Marolf, Henry Maxfield._
- **Replica wormholes** (`open_conjecture`) — New saddle points of the gravitational path integral that account for the entropy decrease. _Proponents: Ahmed Almheiri, Thomas Hartman, Juan Maldacena, Edgar Shaghoulian, Amirhossein Tajdini._
- **Black-hole complementarity** (`open_conjecture`) — Interior and exterior descriptions are complementary, not simultaneously accessible (see #07). _Proponents: Leonard Susskind, Gerard 't Hooft._
- **Soft hair** (`speculative_extension`) — Information stored in soft (zero-energy) graviton/photon modes on the horizon. _Proponents: Stephen Hawking, Malcolm Perry, Andrew Strominger._
- **Information loss is real** (`speculative_extension`) — Bite the bullet: gravity makes evolution non-unitary at the fundamental level (minority view). _Proponents: (various)._
