# #16 — The compact-object mass gaps

*Also known as: lower mass gap, pair-instability mass gap, neutron-star-black-hole gap.*

> **Status: OPEN OBSERVATIONAL** · Statement tier: `observational_constraint` · Resolution tier: `open_conjecture` · First posed: 1990s-present.

## The problem

Two apparent gaps in the compact-object mass distribution are unexplained. The LOWER mass gap (~3-5 M_sun) lies between the heaviest neutron stars and the lightest black holes; X-ray binary data once suggested a real dearth there, but gravitational-wave events (e.g. GW190814's ~2.6 M_sun secondary, GW190425) populate or blur it. The UPPER (pair-instability) gap (~50-120 M_sun) is predicted because pair-instability supernovae leave no remnant; yet GW190521's ~85 M_sun component sits inside it. The open problem: are these gaps real features of stellar/supernova physics, or artifacts of selection and small samples — and what fills them (hierarchical mergers, accretion, exotic channels)?

## Key ideas

- Lower gap (~3-5 M_sun): X-ray binaries showed few objects there; whether it is a true gap depends on the supernova explosion mechanism (rapid vs delayed).
- GW190814's secondary (~2.6 M_sun) is right in the lower gap — neutron star or black hole is unknown.
- Upper (pair-instability) gap (~50-120 M_sun): electron-positron pair production in very massive stellar cores triggers an instability that leaves NO remnant, forbidding direct-collapse BHs in this range.
- GW190521's ~85 M_sun component lies inside the upper gap, suggesting hierarchical (second-generation) mergers or uncertain pair-instability boundaries.
- Selection effects and small samples can mimic or hide a gap; population inference is improving with each LIGO/Virgo/KAGRA run.
- Resolving the gaps constrains supernova physics, the maximum neutron-star mass, and binary-evolution channels.

## Live approaches

- **Gravitational-wave population inference** (`observational_constraint`) — Hierarchically model the merging-binary mass distribution to test for true gaps. _Proponents: Will Farr, Maya Fishbach, LIGO/Virgo/KAGRA Collaboration._
- **Supernova / stellar-evolution modeling** (`numerical_approximation`) — Predict remnant masses (explosion mechanism, pair-instability) and the resulting gaps. _Proponents: Stan Woosley, Alexander Heger._
- **X-ray-binary mass measurements** (`observational_constraint`) — Dynamical masses of compact objects in X-ray binaries probing the lower gap. _Proponents: Charles Bailyn, Feryal Ozel._

- **The Mass Distribution of Stellar Black Holes** (1997) — Charles D. Bailyn, Raj K. Jain, Paolo Coppi, Jerome A. Orosz. _foundational_, `observational_constraint`. https://consensus.app/papers/details/0fb60bd16e8c5bbfb8ee03f70f646cfc/ — ApJ 499, 367. First Bayesian claim of a ~7 Msun clustering of BH masses in transient LMXBs and a significant gap above the neutron-star masses — the original empirical 'lower mass gap'.
- **The Black Hole Mass Distribution in the Galaxy** (2010) — Feryal Ozel, Dimitrios Psaltis, Ramesh Narayan, Jeffrey E. McClintock. _foundational_, `observational_constraint`. https://consensus.app/papers/details/3aace23bdad85048a974781d16c88dbe/ — ApJ 725, 1918. 16 transient LMXBs give a narrow BH mass distribution at 7.8 +/- 1.2 Msun and argue the 2-5 Msun gap is not a selection effect — the canonical lower-gap reference.
- **The Mass Distribution of Stellar-Mass Black Holes** (2010) — Will M. Farr, Niharika Sravan, Andrew Cantrell, Laura Kreidberg, Charles D. Bailyn, Ilya Mandel, Vicky Kalogera. _foundational_, `observational_constraint`. https://consensus.app/papers/details/e00bf6ce45c05638bc733f7ea7f6823c/ — ApJ 741, 103. Bayesian model-selection over 20 X-ray binaries; finds M(1%) > 4.3-4.5 Msun, providing strong statistical evidence for the lower gap.
- **Compact Remnant Mass Function: Dependence on the Explosion Mechanism and Metallicity** (2011) — Chris L. Fryer, Krzysztof Belczynski, Grzegorz Wiktorowicz, Michal Dominik, Vicky Kalogera, Daniel E. Holz. _foundational_, `open_conjecture`. https://consensus.app/papers/details/a64a47cdc9cd554db057644c792c17db/ — ApJ 749, 91. The 'rapid' vs 'delayed' supernova-engine remnant-mass prescriptions used by essentially all population-synthesis codes; rapid engine produces a lower mass gap, delayed fills it.
- **Missing Black Holes Unveil the Supernova Explosion Mechanism** (2012) — Krzysztof Belczynski, Grzegorz Wiktorowicz, Chris L. Fryer, Daniel E. Holz, Vicky Kalogera. _foundational_, `open_conjecture`. https://consensus.app/papers/details/917e9bd73b71580f8912dead122075fb/ — ApJ 757, 91. Argues the observed gap implies the core-collapse explosion is launched within ~100-200 ms by a rapidly growing instability; a fill-in would instead imply slower (>200 ms) growth.
- **Mass Measurements of Black Holes in X-Ray Transients: Is There a Mass Gap?** (2012) — Laura Kreidberg, Charles D. Bailyn, Will M. Farr, Vicky Kalogera. _foundational_, `observational_constraint`. https://consensus.app/papers/details/a35a6ab2c89956dca74abf57903906cd/ — ApJ 757, 36. Systematic-error study of inclination/ellipsoidal modeling showing some gap candidates (e.g. GRO J0422+32) may move into the gap — a key caveat on the empirical gap.
- **Very Massive Stars, Pair-Instability Supernovae and Intermediate-Mass Black Holes with the SEVN Code** (2017) — Mario Spera, Michela Mapelli. _foundational_, `open_conjecture`. https://consensus.app/papers/details/dc5c67c208fa5b8c8997fb16b408d1f3/ — MNRAS 470, 4739. Implements PPISN/PISN in population synthesis, predicting an upper BH mass gap roughly 60-120 Msun — an early quantitative upper-gap modeling reference.
- **A Noninteracting Low-Mass Black Hole-Giant Star Binary System** (2019) — Todd A. Thompson, Christopher S. Kochanek, Krzysztof Z. Stanek, et al.. _recent_, `observational_constraint`. https://consensus.app/papers/details/8bc5f7f5a90a5d8781b9f993664ac462/ — Science 366, 637. 2MASS J05215658+4359220: a ~3.3 Msun unseen companion to a giant — a candidate non-interacting lower-gap object (its nature is debated; see van den Heuvel comment).
- **Pulsational Pair-Instability Supernovae. I. Pre-collapse Evolution and Pulsational Mass Ejection** (2019) — Shing-Chi Leung, Ken'ichi Nomoto, Sergei Blinnikov. _foundational_, `open_conjecture`. https://consensus.app/papers/details/2d891205fb0d5a8d9bcefb75f0dd1bde/ — ApJ 887, 72. Detailed hydrodynamic PPISN models setting the maximum BH mass below the upper gap near ~50 Msun, consistent with early LIGO/Virgo BH masses.
- **The Impact of Pair-Instability Mass Loss on the Binary Black Hole Mass Distribution** (2019) — Simon Stevenson, Matthew Sampson, Jade Powell, Alejandro Vigna-Gomez, Coenraad J. COMPAS isolated-binary models predicting a pile-up at 30-45 Msun and a cutoff near ~45 Msun from (P)PISN — an early link of the upper-gap edge to GW data.
- **GW190814: Gravitational Waves from the Coalescence of a 23 Solar Mass Black Hole with a 2.6 Solar Mass Compact Object** (2020) — LIGO Scientific Collaboration, Virgo Collaboration, R. - **Exploring the Lower Mass Gap and Unequal Mass Regime in Compact Binary Evolution** (2020) — Michael Zevin, Mario Spera, Christopher P. L. Population modeling of GW190814-like systems; concludes that forming them requires a delayed/long-timescale engine, hinting the lower gap may be narrow or absent.
- **A Lower Bound on the Maximum Mass if the Secondary in GW190814 Was Once a Rapidly Spinning Neutron Star** (2020) — Elias R. Most, Lukas R. Weih, Luciano Rezzolla, Jurgen Schaffner-Bielich. _recent_, `analytic_classical`. https://consensus.app/papers/details/67dd9623dd995693b3144249d49b98a5/ — MNRAS 499, L82. Uses universal relations to argue GW190814's secondary could be a (formerly) rapidly rotating NS, yielding M(TOV) > 2.08 Msun — links lower gap to the NS EoS.
- **GW190521: A Binary Black Hole Merger with a Total Mass of 150 Solar Masses** (2020) — LIGO Scientific Collaboration, Virgo Collaboration, R. Abbott, et al.. _recent_, `observational_constraint`. https://consensus.app/papers/details/1fcc328364b85c538e9e2b39d11297c0/ — PRL 125, 101102. Primary ~85 Msun lies in the pair-instability (upper) mass gap with 99% probability; remnant ~142 Msun is an intermediate-mass black hole.
- **Properties and Astrophysical Implications of the 150 Msun Binary Black Hole Merger GW190521** (2020) — LIGO Scientific Collaboration, Virgo Collaboration, R. Abbott, et al.. _recent_, `open_conjecture`. https://consensus.app/papers/details/6b36d7304b2f593b8cf5b447d3dd2859/ — ApJL 900, L13. Companion astrophysics paper discussing channels to populate the upper gap: stellar mergers, hierarchical mergers in clusters/AGN disks, and exotic alternatives.
- **Constraints from Gravitational-Wave Detections of Binary Black Hole Mergers on the 12C(alpha,gamma)16O Rate** (2020) — Robert Farmer, Mathieu Renzo, Selma E. de Mink, Mario Spera, Stephen Justham. _recent_, `open_conjecture`. https://consensus.app/papers/details/2335c29f94da584ba8458757cc1934e1/ — ApJL 902, L36. Shows the lower edge of the upper gap is set mainly by the uncertain 12C(alpha,gamma)16O reaction rate; GW BH masses can in turn constrain that nuclear S-factor.
- **Formation of GW190521 from Stellar Evolution: the Impact of the Hydrogen-Rich Envelope, Dredge-Up, and 12C(alpha,gamma)16O Rate on the Pair-Instability Black Hole Mass Gap** (2020) — Guglielmo Costa, Alessandro Bressan, Michela Mapelli, Paola Marigo, Giuliano Iorio, Mario Spera. _recent_, `open_conjecture`. https://consensus.app/papers/details/3d51882a59d959fcb5656b9be18c876f/ — MNRAS 501, 4514. Demonstrates the upper-gap lower edge can shift to ~70 Msun (or close entirely) given envelope collapse, dredge-up, and reaction-rate uncertainties — a model route to GW190521.
- **The Impact of Stellar Rotation on the Black Hole Mass-Gap from Pair-Instability Supernovae** (2020) — Pablo Marchant, Takashi J. Moriya. _recent_, `open_conjecture`. https://consensus.app/papers/details/e7d85d7943be5c7f80caba5b8b1df371/ — A&A 640, L18. Shows the lower edge of the upper gap depends on rotation and angular-momentum transport (Spruit-Tayler dynamo), predicting a BH-spin dependence of the gap edge.
- **Population Properties of Compact Objects from the Second LIGO-Virgo Gravitational-Wave Transient Catalog / Population of Merging Compact Binaries Inferred Using Gravitational Waves through GWTC-3** (2021) — LIGO Scientific Collaboration, Virgo Collaboration, KAGRA Collaboration, R. Abbott, et al.. _recent_, `observational_constraint`. https://consensus.app/papers/details/441e20a0ac995d79b69037079c5e6e4f/ — PRX 13, 011048 (GWTC-3 population). Cannot yet confirm or rule out the lower gap; finds BH mass-spectrum features (peaks near chirp mass 8.3 and 27.9 Msun) and no sharp cutoff above ~60 Msun.
- **Observation of Gravitational Waves from Two Neutron Star-Black Hole Coalescences** (2021) — LIGO Scientific Collaboration, Virgo Collaboration, KAGRA Collaboration, R. Abbott, et al.. _recent_, `observational_constraint`. https://consensus.app/papers/details/3843162c55a253cb999af5d9d12c046b/ — ApJL 915, L5. GW200105 and GW200115, the first confident NS-BH mergers; their BH primaries (~5.7-8.9 Msun) and NS secondaries probe the edges of the lower gap.
- **Bridging the Gap: Categorizing Gravitational-Wave Events at the Transition between Neutron Stars and Black Holes** (2021) — Amanda M. Farah, Maya Fishbach, Reed Essick, Daniel E. Population analysis placing the (tentative) lower-gap edges at ~2.2 and ~6.0 Msun and self-consistently classifying GW190814, GW200105, GW200115 at the NS-BH transition.

Review of hierarchical (second+ generation) BH mergers as a way to populate the pair-instability gap, with the spin~0.7 signature and escape-speed requirements.
- **The Pair-Instability Mass Gap for Black Holes** (2021) — Stan E. Woosley, Alexander Heger. _review_, `open_conjecture`. https://consensus.app/papers/details/15061604f26a56279f3241521d1637d2/ — ApJL 912, L31. Examines four factors (reaction rates, binary stripping, rotation, super-Eddington accretion) that can move the upper-gap boundaries, allowing M_low up to ~64-70 Msun.
- **Observation of Gravitational Waves from the Coalescence of a 2.5-4.5 Msun Compact Object and a Neutron Star (GW230529)** (2024) — LIGO Scientific Collaboration, Virgo Collaboration, KAGRA Collaboration, A. G. Abac, et al.. _recent_, `observational_constraint`. https://consensus.app/papers/details/0dec0b4a72905897be52aa2f38b0d929/ — ApJL 970, L34. GW230529: a 2.5-4.5 Msun primary most plausibly a lower-mass-gap black hole merging with a neutron star — strongest GW evidence yet for objects inside the lower gap.

## Key physicists

- **Charles D. Bailyn** (originator, 1990s-present, Yale University) — Led the 1997 dynamical-mass study of transient LMXBs that first identified the empirical lower mass gap between neutron stars and black holes.
- **Feryal Ozel** (foundational, 2000s-present, Georgia Institute of Technology (formerly University of Arizona)) — Authored the 2010 Galactic BH mass-distribution paper establishing the narrow ~7.8 Msun BH peak and arguing the 2-5 Msun gap is physical, not a selection effect.
- **Vicky Kalogera** (foundational, 1990s-present, Northwestern University (CIERA)) — Co-developed compact-binary population synthesis and the statistical/theoretical framing of the mass gap and the supernova-engine remnant-mass prescriptions.
- **Chris L. Fryer** (foundational, 1990s-present, Los Alamos National Laboratory) — Built the rapid- vs delayed-explosion remnant-mass formulae (Fryer et al. 2012) that determine whether population-synthesis models produce or fill the lower mass gap.
- **Krzysztof Belczynski** (foundational, 2000s-present, Nicolaus Copernicus Astronomical Center, Warsaw) — Argued (2012) that the 'missing' low-mass black holes constrain the timescale of the core-collapse supernova explosion mechanism.
- **Stan E. Woosley** (originator, 1970s-present, University of California, Santa Cruz) — Pioneered the stellar-evolution theory of pair-instability and pulsational-pair-instability supernovae that creates the upper (pair-instability) black-hole mass gap.
- **Alexander Heger** (foundational, 1990s-present, Monash University) — Co-developed massive-star and pair-instability supernova models defining the location and uncertainties of the ~50-130 Msun upper mass gap.
- **Maya Fishbach** (active, 2010s-present, University of Toronto / CITA) — Leads gravitational-wave population inference probing both gaps, including hierarchical-merger signatures and categorizing events at the NS-BH transition.
- **Michela Mapelli** (active, 2010s-present, University of Padova / Heidelberg University) — Developed the SEVN population-synthesis treatment of PPISN/PISN and dynamical formation channels that populate the upper mass gap.
- **Robert Farmer** (active, 2010s-present, Max Planck Institute for Astrophysics / University of Amsterdam) — Showed the upper-gap lower edge is governed by the 12C(alpha,gamma)16O reaction rate, tying gravitational-wave BH masses to nuclear astrophysics.
