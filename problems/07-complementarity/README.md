# #07 — Black-hole complementarity & its consistency

*Also known as: horizon complementarity, stretched horizon, STU complementarity.*

> **Status: CONTESTED** · Statement tier: `open_conjecture` · Resolution tier: `open_conjecture` · First posed: 1993.

## The problem

Black-hole complementarity (Susskind, Thorlacius, Uglum 1993; 't Hooft) proposes that the descriptions seen by an infalling observer (who passes a smooth horizon) and an external observer (who sees information thermalized and re-emitted on a 'stretched horizon') are complementary, not contradictory, because no single observer can access both. It was the leading reconciliation of unitarity with a smooth horizon until the firewall argument (#06) challenged its consistency. Whether complementarity is internally consistent, and how it is realized, is open.

## Key ideas

- No-cloning is respected because no observer sees information both inside and in the radiation.
- The 'stretched horizon' is a fictitious membrane (a Planck length outside the true horizon) that absorbs, thermalizes and re-radiates information for the external observer.
- Complementarity underlies and motivated the holographic principle.
- Hayden-Preskill: a black hole that has already radiated half its entropy returns infalling information very quickly (the black hole as a near-optimal scrambler).
- The firewall argument (#06) claims complementarity is inconsistent for old black holes; ER=EPR (#08) is a proposed rescue.
- AdS/CFT gives a manifestly unitary external description, sharpening the question of how the interior is encoded.

## Live approaches

- **Stretched-horizon membrane** (`open_conjecture`) — Model the horizon as a dissipative membrane for external observers (the membrane paradigm made quantum). _Proponents: Leonard Susskind, Larus Thorlacius, John Uglum._
- **Holographic encoding (AdS/CFT)** (`open_conjecture`) — The interior is encoded in boundary CFT data; subregion/entanglement-wedge reconstruction. _Proponents: Juan Maldacena, Daniel Harlow._
- **Fast scrambling / Hayden-Preskill** (`open_conjecture`) — Quantify how quickly information is returned, constraining when complementarity must hold. _Proponents: Patrick Hayden, John Preskill, Yasuhiro Sekino, Leonard Susskind._

- **Dimensional reduction in quantum gravity** (1993) — Gerard 't Hooft. _foundational_, `open_conjecture`. `arXiv:gr-qc/9310026` — The original holographic-principle proposal (Salamfestschrift, 1993; gr-qc/9310026). 't Hooft argued the degrees of freedom in a region scale with boundary area, motivating horizon-information ideas behind complementarity. Known classic, not surfaced by the search engine in original form.
- **Particle creation by black holes** (1975) — Stephen W. Hawking. _foundational_, `analytic_classical`. doi:10.1007/BF02345020 — Commun. Math. Phys. 43, 199 (1975). The thermal-emission result that created the information paradox complementarity tries to resolve. Canonical landmark cited from certain knowledge.
- **Black holes and entropy** (1973) — Jacob D. Bekenstein. _foundational_, `analytic_classical`. doi:10.1103/PhysRevD.7.2333 — Phys. Rev. D 7, 2333 (1973). Horizon entropy proportional to area — the counting later interpreted holographically. Canonical landmark cited from certain knowledge.
- **Information in the holographic universe / Average entropy of a subsystem (Information in black hole radiation)** (1993) — Don N. Page. _foundational_, `open_conjecture`. `arXiv:hep-th/9306083` — Page, 'Information in black hole radiation', Phys. Rev. Lett. 71, 3743 (1993); hep-th/9306083. Introduced the Page time and Page curve central to any unitarity-restoring scenario. Canonical landmark cited from certain knowledge.
- **Black holes as mirrors: quantum information in random subsystems (Hayden-Preskill)** (2007) — Patrick Hayden, John Preskill. _foundational_, `open_conjecture`. `arXiv:0708.4025` — JHEP 0709:120 (2007); arXiv:0708.4025. The 'black hole as a mirror' thought experiment: information dumped into an old black hole returns rapidly if the dynamics scramble. Canonical landmark cited from certain knowledge (search surfaced derivative follow-ups, not the original).
- **Fast Scramblers** (2008) — Yasuhiro Sekino, Leonard Susskind. _foundational_, `open_conjecture`. `arXiv:0808.2096` — JHEP 0810:065 (2008); arXiv:0808.2096. The fast-scrambling conjecture: black holes scramble in time ~ (beta/2pi) log S, the fastest in nature. Canonical landmark cited from certain knowledge.
- **Towards the fast scrambling conjecture** (2011) — Nima Lashkari, Douglas Stanford, Matthew Hastings, Tobias Osborne, Patrick Hayden. _foundational_, `open_conjecture`. `arXiv:1111.6580` — JHEP 04 (2013) 022. Lieb-Robinson lower bound on scrambling time plus model examples; key technical support for the fast-scrambler conjecture.
- **Black holes: complementarity or firewalls? (AMPS)** (2012) — Ahmed Almheiri, Donald Marolf, Joseph Polchinski, James Sully. _foundational_, `open_conjecture`. `arXiv:1207.3123` — JHEP 02 (2013) 062. The firewall argument: the three complementarity-friendly postulates are mutually inconsistent for an old black hole. The central modern challenge to naive complementarity.
- **An apologia for firewalls (AMPSS)** (2013) — Ahmed Almheiri, Donald Marolf, Joseph Polchinski, Douglas Stanford, James Sully. _foundational_, `open_conjecture`. `arXiv:1304.6483` — JHEP 09 (2013) 018. Sharpens the firewall argument and rebuts proposed escapes, including identifying interior with exterior degrees of freedom.
- **Gauge-gravity duality and the black hole interior** (2013) — Donald Marolf, Joseph Polchinski. _foundational_, `open_conjecture`. `arXiv:1307.4706` — Phys. Rev. Lett. 111, 171301 (2013). Entanglement-independent firewall argument for typical states in AdS/CFT; addresses ER=EPR.
- **Comments on black holes I: the possibility of complementarity (fuzzball complementarity)** (2012) — Samir D. Mathur, David Turton. _foundational_, `open_conjecture`. `arXiv:1208.2005` — JHEP 01 (2014) 034. Response to AMPS proposing 'fuzzball complementarity' as a string-theory alternative; statistical identity of infalling measurements outside the horizon.
- **Cool horizons for entangled black holes (ER=EPR)** (2013) — Juan Maldacena, Leonard Susskind. _foundational_, `open_conjecture`. `arXiv:1306.0533` — Fortschr. Phys. 61, 781 (2013). The ER=EPR conjecture: entanglement is geometrized as Einstein-Rosen bridges, proposed to defuse the firewall and reconcile complementarity.
- **The Large-N Limit of Superconformal Field Theories and Supergravity (AdS/CFT)** (1997) — Juan Maldacena. _foundational_, `open_conjecture`. `arXiv:hep-th/9711200` — Adv. Theor. Math. Phys. 2, 231 (1998). The holographic duality that gives a concrete, manifestly unitary boundary description of a gravitating bulk — the strongest evidence that black-hole evolution is unitary, underpinning complementarity.
- **Quantum computation vs. firewalls (Harlow-Hayden)** (2013) — Daniel Harlow, Patrick Hayden. _foundational_, `open_conjecture`. `arXiv:1301.4504` — JHEP 06 (2013) 085; arXiv:1301.4504. Argues that distilling the entanglement needed to detect a firewall is computationally intractable (exponential in the entropy), suggesting complementarity is protected by computational complexity. Canonical landmark cited from certain knowledge; search surfaced follow-ups (Python's Lunch, Weinstein review) rather than the original.
- **A bound on chaos (Maldacena-Shenker-Stanford)** (2015) — Juan Maldacena, Stephen H. Shenker, Douglas Stanford. _foundational_, `open_conjecture`. `arXiv:1503.01409` — JHEP 08 (2016) 106; arXiv:1503.01409. Universal bound lambda_L <= 2 pi / beta on the Lyapunov/OTOC growth rate, saturated by black holes — the precise sense in which black holes are maximal scramblers. Canonical landmark cited from certain knowledge (search returned derivative OTOC papers).
- **Black holes and the butterfly effect** (2013) — Stephen H. Shenker, Douglas Stanford. _foundational_, `open_conjecture`. `arXiv:1306.0622` — JHEP 03 (2014) 067; arXiv:1306.0622. Shock-wave/OTOC analysis showing horizon dynamics scramble perturbations exponentially fast — the gravitational mechanism behind fast scrambling. Canonical landmark cited from certain knowledge.
- **Efficient decoding for the Hayden-Preskill protocol** (2017) — Beni Yoshida, Alexei Kitaev. _recent_, `open_conjecture`. `arXiv:1710.03363` — arXiv:1710.03363. Explicit (probabilistic and Grover-based) decoding algorithms recovering information from Hawking radiation, tying scrambling/OTOC decay to information recovery.
- **The Page curve of Hawking radiation from semiclassical geometry (islands)** (2019) — Ahmed Almheiri, Raghu Mahajan, Juan Maldacena, Ying Zhao. _recent_, `open_conjecture`. `arXiv:1908.10996` — JHEP 03 (2020) 149. Introduces the island rule: the radiation's entanglement wedge includes the black-hole interior, yielding a unitary Page curve. (Consensus listed grouped AHMSZ authorship; primary authors Almheiri-Mahajan-Maldacena-Zhao.)
- **Entanglement wedge reconstruction and the information paradox** (2019) — Geoffrey Penington. _recent_, `open_conjecture`. `arXiv:1905.08255` — JHEP 09 (2020) 002. Quantum-extremal-surface phase transition at the Page time gives the Page curve and recovers Hayden-Preskill via entanglement-wedge reconstruction, without a firewall.
- **Replica wormholes and the entropy of Hawking radiation** (2019) — Ahmed Almheiri, Thomas Hartman, Juan Maldacena, Edgar Shaghoulian, Amirhossein Tajdini. _recent_, `open_conjecture`. `arXiv:1911.12333` — JHEP 05 (2020) 013. New gravitational-path-integral saddles (replica wormholes) reproduce the island rule and the unitary Page curve in JT gravity.
- **The Python's Lunch: geometric obstructions to decoding Hawking radiation** (2019) — Adam R. Brown, Hrant Gharibyan, Geoff Penington, Leonard Susskind. _recent_, `open_conjecture`. `arXiv:1912.00228` — JHEP 08 (2020) 121. Geometrizes Harlow-Hayden computational hardness as a wormhole obstruction (the 'Python's Lunch') between non-minimal quantum extremal surfaces.
- **The ghost in the radiation: robust encodings of the black hole interior** (2020) — Isaac H. Kim, Eugene Tang, John Preskill. _recent_, `open_conjecture`. `arXiv:2003.05451` — JHEP 06 (2020) 031. Pseudorandomness + quantum error correction protect the interior from computationally bounded observers, sharpening the complexity-based defense of complementarity.
- **The entropy of Hawking radiation (review)** (2020) — Ahmed Almheiri, Thomas Hartman, Juan Maldacena, Edgar Shaghoulian, Amirhossein Tajdini. _review_, `open_conjecture`. `arXiv:2006.06872` — Rev. Mod. Phys. 93, 035002 (2021). The definitive recent review of fine-grained gravitational entropy, islands, and the unitary Page curve for Hawking radiation.
- **Complementarity for a dynamical black hole** (2024) — Bryce Concepcion, Adam Levine, et al.. _recent_, `open_conjecture`. doi:10.1103/PhysRevD.110 — Phys. Rev. D (2024). Recent reformulation arguing the interior lies in the causal past of the radiation, so no operation on the radiation can affect an infalling observer — a modern defense of complementarity using the stretched horizon.
