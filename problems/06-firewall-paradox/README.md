# #06 — The firewall paradox (AMPS)

*Also known as: AMPS paradox, firewalls, black hole complementarity vs firewalls.*

> **Status: OPEN** · Statement tier: `open_conjecture` · Resolution tier: `open_conjecture` · First posed: 2012.

## The problem

Almheiri, Marolf, Polchinski and Sully (2012) argued that three widely-held assumptions cannot all be true for an old (post-Page-time) black hole: (i) evaporation is unitary, (ii) effective field theory is valid outside the horizon, and (iii) an infalling observer sees nothing special at the horizon (the equivalence principle / 'no drama'). Monogamy of entanglement forces a contradiction; the proposed escape is that the horizon is replaced by a high-energy 'firewall' that destroys infalling observers, violating the equivalence principle. Whether firewalls exist, or which assumption truly fails, is open.

## Key ideas

- Monogamy of entanglement: a late Hawking quantum cannot be maximally entangled with both its early-radiation partner (required by unitarity) and its interior partner (required by a smooth horizon).
- Page time: the argument bites only for 'old' black holes that have radiated more than half their entropy.
- Firewall: dropping the smooth-horizon assumption replaces the horizon with a wall of high-energy quanta.
- Mathur's earlier 'small corrections' theorem reached a related conclusion via fuzzballs.
- ER=EPR (problem #08) was proposed partly to dissolve the firewall by identifying the two entanglements geometrically.
- The island/replica resolution of #02 bears directly on whether a firewall is required.

## Live approaches

- **Firewalls** (`open_conjecture`) — Accept that old black-hole horizons are not smooth; the interior is cut off by a high-energy surface. _Proponents: Almheiri, Marolf, Polchinski, Sully, Bousso._
- **ER=EPR / state-dependence** (`open_conjecture`) — Identify interior and exterior partners so monogamy is not violated; interior operators depend on the state. _Proponents: Juan Maldacena, Leonard Susskind, Kyriakos Papadodimas, Suvrat Raju._
- **Computational complexity / firewall avoidance** (`open_conjecture`) — Decoding the interior is exponentially hard, so a firewall is never operationally encountered (Harlow-Hayden). _Proponents: Daniel Harlow, Patrick Hayden._
- **Fuzzballs** (`speculative_extension`) — There is no smooth interior at all; the horizon is the surface of a stringy bound state. _Proponents: Samir Mathur._
