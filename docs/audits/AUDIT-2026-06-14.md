# Audit — 2026-06-14 (initial catalogue review)

A self-audit of the catalogue against its own charter (PHILOSOPHY.md). The point
is to state honestly what is solid and what is not.

## Scope reviewed

All 16 problems (`problems/01..16`), the schema, the validators, the RAG build,
and the governance docs.

## What passes

- **Structure & schema (PASS).** All 16 `problem.json` validate against
  `schema/problem.schema.json`; ranks are unique and contiguous 1–16; every
  folder has both `README.md` and `problem.json`; `slug` matches folder and rank.
  `scripts/validate.py` enforces this and is green in CI.
- **RAG reproducibility (PASS).** `rag/INDEX.jsonl` (198 records) is regenerated
  deterministically by `build_rag.py`; CI fails on drift. LF is pinned via
  `.gitattributes` so Windows↔Linux line endings cannot cause false drift.
- **Citation integrity (PASS, by construction).** Every paper has either a
  resolvable identifier (arXiv/DOI/URL) or a bibliographic `note`; the validator
  rejects a bare, unverifiable citation. No citation was fabricated. Famous
  landmark papers are recorded with author/year/journal; a few arXiv ids for
  pre-2000 works are given as the abstract id and flagged for verification.
- **Truth-tier honesty (PASS).** Every problem's resolution is labelled
  `open_conjecture` or `speculative_extension`; nothing is claimed solved. The
  one partial result (Strominger–Vafa for SUSY/extremal entropy, #03) is scoped
  precisely.

## Known gaps & limitations (honest)

1. **Citation coverage is SEED, not the "25 papers each" aspiration.** Each
   problem currently carries ~4–7 verified key references, not 25. This is
   deliberate: hitting 25 would have required fabricating or low-confidence
   entries, which the charter forbids. `metadata.coverage_note` says so per
   problem. *Remediation:* a sourced enrichment pass (arXiv/Consensus/ADS) — the
   automated 16-agent research workflow hit transient server rate-limits twice on
   2026-06-14; rerun when clear, or expand manually.
2. **Physicist lists are ~6–9 each, not 10.** Same reasoning; only confidently-
   real people with a real contribution are listed.
3. **A few pre-2000 arXiv identifiers are unverified.** Flagged in notes; treat
   as bibliographic pointers until checked against the arXiv/ADS record.
4. **Ranking is editorial.** The depth/age/tractability scores are defensible
   judgments, not measurements (PHILOSOPHY §4); reasonable experts would reorder
   at the margins (e.g. #06 firewall vs #03 entropy). Disputes go in KANBAN.
5. **No problem is exhaustively "complete."** New sub-problems exist (holographic
   complexity growth, islands in cosmology, the Weak Gravity Conjecture's BH
   motivation); candidates are tracked in KANBAN before adding (avoid duplicates).

## Verdict

The catalogue is **structurally complete and integrity-clean** at seed depth.
The honest next step is citation/physicist **enrichment**, not restructuring.
